100 YEARS of QUEER...

HISTORY

Welcome to an evolving exploration of queer history over the last century. This interactive map is a dynamic tool for academic research and educational purposes. It compiles data from various sources, reflecting the rich tapestry of queer communities and milestones. As this project is continually updated, we encourage users to approach the data with an understanding of its provisional nature and look forward to contributions that can enrich its accuracy and depth.

The map draws on data from the following key sources:

  • "Queer Heroes NW History Walking Tour." Gay & Lesbian Archives of the Pacific Northwest, www.glapn.org/6045walkingtour.html. Accessed May, 2013.
  • Boag, Peter. (2004). "Does Portland Need a Homophile Society?" Gay Culture and Activism in the Rose City between World War II and Stonewall. Oregon historical quarterly. Oregon Historical Society. 105. 6-39. 10.1353/ohq.2004.0066.
  • "Collections." Gay & Lesbian Archives of the Pacific Northwest, www.glapn.org/5000collections.html

Please exercise caution and critical judgment when engaging with the data, keeping in mind the need for further verification. Contributions and corrections are warmly invited to enhance the accuracy and comprehensiveness of this resource.
